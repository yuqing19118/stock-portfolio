Guardrails

Rules before trades.

  • No margin. Borrowed money turns volatility into forced selling.
  • No weekly options. Treat short-dated options like lottery tickets, not a plan.
  • Options are defined-risk. Prefer LEAPS or debit spreads; never naked short options.
  • Use ETFs first. They reduce single-company blowup risk while still giving targeted exposure.
  • Fractional shares are fine. If your broker only allows whole shares, round down and leave the extra in SGOV.
  • Rebalance winners. If a risky sleeve doubles, harvest part of it.

Weekly Retrospective

Friday after market close: update prices, ask what changed, and resize only when the thesis or risk changes.

  • What worked? Which sleeve carried returns, and was that skill or market beta?
  • What hurt? Which drawdown was expected, and which one exposed a bad assumption?
  • What changed? Earnings, rates, valuation, liquidity, sentiment, or nothing meaningful?
  • What action? Hold, trim, add, or move risk back into the core.

  • CSX — in queue because the rule scan produced a high-confidence paper buy; compare railroad trend strength against industrial/cyclical risk.
  • QQQ / SMH — in queue because they may express growth/semiconductor exposure with less single-stock risk.
  • Exit rule — leave the queue if confidence falls below 55%, thesis is invalidated, stop is hit, data quality is weak, or a cleaner expression replaces it.

Strategy Lab

  • Momentum + quality — buy only when trend strength and business quality agree.
  • Options overlays — paper test debit spreads separately from stock signals.
  • Alpha gate — no stronger alerts unless paper performance beats SPY.

What I Learned

  • Baseline first — absolute gains do not prove skill if SPY did better.
  • Risk budget matters — options need premium caps, not vibes.
  • Rule-only mode is limited — qualitative research needs filings, earnings, and transcripts.

Why I Changed My Mind

  • From static portfolio to agent — the goal shifted from allocation plan to measurable learning loop.
  • From return to alpha — performance is judged against SPY, not just dollar gains.
  • From options as speed to options as experiment — paper-only until evidence improves.
  • From fixed queue to dynamic queue — queue membership changes when signals, risk, alpha, or thesis quality changes.

Do Not Trade Yet Warnings

  • No real trades from one scan — paper evidence is too thin.
  • No naked options — undefined risk is outside the system.
  • No trade without a review note — every alert needs thesis, risk, stop, and invalidation.
